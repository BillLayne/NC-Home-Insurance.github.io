Home Insurance Coverage Types Explained

Understanding the six main coverage types helps you make informed decisions about protecting your home.

Coverage A - Dwelling

Protects your home's structure and built-in appliances from covered damages like fire, wind, and hail. This should cover the cost to rebuild your entire home.

Example: If a tree falls on your Elkin home during a storm, Coverage A pays to repair the roof and structural damage.

Coverage B - Other Structures

Covers detached structures like garages, sheds, and fences. Usually 10% of your dwelling coverage amount.

Example: Wind damage to your detached garage or garden shed would be covered under Coverage B.

Coverage C - Personal Property

Protects your belongings like furniture, clothing, and electronics. Typically 50-70% of your dwelling coverage.

Example: If your furniture is damaged by water from a burst pipe, Coverage C helps replace it.

Coverage D - Loss of Use

Pays for temporary living expenses if your home becomes uninhabitable due to a covered loss.

Example: Hotel and restaurant costs while your home is being repaired after storm damage.

Coverage E - Personal Liability

Protects you if someone is injured on your property or you damage someone else's property. Minimum $100,000 recommended.

Example: A delivery person slips on your icy walkway and needs medical attention.

Coverage F - Medical Payments

Covers medical expenses for guests injured on your property, regardless of fault. Usually $1,000-$5,000.

Example: A neighbor's child gets hurt playing in your yard and needs emergency care.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about home insurance in North Carolina

Home insurance in NC typically covers your dwelling, personal property, liability, and additional living expenses. However, it doesn't cover floods - you need separate flood insurance for that protection.

The average cost is $1,545 per year, but your rate depends on your home's value, location, and coverage levels. Elkin residents may pay different rates based on local risk factors like proximity to rivers and historical weather patterns.

While not required by state law, flood insurance is highly recommended. Even areas not in flood zones can experience flooding from heavy rains or storms. The Yadkin River system can cause unexpected flooding in our area.

Contact your insurance company immediately to report the claim. Document all damage with photos, make temporary repairs to prevent further damage, and keep all receipts. Bill Layne Insurance helps guide you through this process.

Standard policies don't cover floods, earthquakes, normal wear and tear, or maintenance issues. Some policies also exclude certain types of water damage or require separate coverage for expensive items like jewelry.
